The browser, Mozilla Firefox

Written by: Nathan van de Kolk, Joran van de Kolk, Do Toan Nguyen

History

Summary

The Mozilla project (see fig 1) started in 1998 by releasing the Netscape browser suite source code. The open-source nature of the project is primarily commendable. After multiple years of development, Mozilla 1.0, released in the year 2002. In the same year the first version of Phoenix, later renamed to Firefox, was released for many people unnoticed. At the moment, more than 90% of the internet browser market was taken by Internet Explorer.
Later on, in 2004, Firefox 1.0 was released and succesfully, downloads reached numbers over 100 million. Since then, new versions were frequent and FireFox is still one of the major internetbrowsers in the market. Since its inception, Firefox' goal was to bring choice to the user [1].

Usage Share

Firefox usage was at a peak at the fall of 2009 when its share of the browser market consisted of an impressive 32.21%, taking over the position of Internet Explorer 7. Howerever, usage has declined ever since due to a new competitor: Google Chrome. Currently, Firefox has a usage share of around 4 percent, while Chrome's is more in the range of 60-65. Making Firefox the third-most popular webbrowser after Chrome and Safari [3].

FireFox OS

FireFox OS was an open-source operating system for mobile phones being developed by the Mozilla Project. It has since been cancelled. It was built using 3 layers: the applicationlayer, Open Web Platform Interface and the infrastucturelayer (Gonk).
The first layer consisted of a combination of HTML5, JavaScript and CSS. The second contained all of the technological stuff: application programming interfaces. These communicate with the layout-engine known as Gecko. The final layer is the actual operating system which was based on linux [4].

Other Platforms

The latest verion of Firefox is currently available for the following platforms:

  • Microsoft Windows 7 or later;
  • MacOS 10.12 or later;
  • Android 5.0 and later;
  • iOS 11.4 and later;
  • Linux desktop;

Older versions of these platforms may also have an old version of Firefox available, but those versions are no longer supported.

Features

Default blocking of trackers

According to the official websites, Ads are distracting and make the browsingexperience more sluggish with slower loadtimes. Thus the blocking of trackers is of by default without the need of fiddling in the security settings. [5].

Password Management

Just as in prevelant on other browser the option of saving one's passwords is a feature. Due to the connection of the browser across multiple devices these can also be shared, making the transition between devices just that little bit less intrusive. [5].

OS and language availability

One of Firefox's more unique properties is its availabilty on several Operating systems. It is also available in over 90 languages. [5].
